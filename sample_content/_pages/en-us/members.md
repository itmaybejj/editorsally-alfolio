## Why

The future of Editoria11y is a cooperative. Members will help drive the project forward, and get license keys to unlock the features sponsored by the community.

We speak of members rather than customers, because we have structured the issue credit and bug bounty system to mean that many of the "premium" users may never need to contribute financially.

Those supporting the project can expect priority support and weighted voted in the issue queues.

## Subscriptions

The Drupal and WordPress modules will both soon have the option of a premium tier, with community-supported features for developers that complement the core editorial tests. 

For the foreseeable future these plugins will follow a [pay what you want](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pay_what_you_want) model, with suggested contributions of $10/month/site.

## Issue credits and bug bounties

Finding bugs helps the project move forward.

Fixing bugs helps the project move forward quickly!

And healthy communities need good documentation and accurate translations.

Those making any of these sorts of meaningful contributions can check in with the module maintainers for subscription credits commensurate with their contributed time.

## Direct sponsorships

Small subscriptions make for steady but slow progress.

Project users that want a feature developed quickly have the option of taking it on themselves or covering the cost of hiring developers. These costs are credited against future subscription costs.
